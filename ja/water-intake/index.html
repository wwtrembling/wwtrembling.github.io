Estimate only. Real needs vary by sex, body composition, sweat rate, kidney function, and altitude. Drink to thirst; pale-yellow urine is the best practical indicator.

How to use

  1. Enter your body weight in kilograms.
  2. Pick activity level and climate. Heavy exercise adds 500 ml; hot climates add another 500 ml.
  3. Click Calculate — the daily target appears in ml, liters, and 250 ml glasses.

Formula: 33 ml × weight(kg), plus 350 ml for moderate exercise / 500 ml for heavy, plus 500 ml for hot climates. Caffeinated drinks count toward intake (not as much as water but most of the volume); alcohol is a net negative for hydration.

This estimate covers total fluid — food contributes about 20% of daily water (more if you eat lots of fruit, soup, or vegetables). Drinking ~1.5–2.5 L of water-equivalent fluids on top of food usually covers it.
